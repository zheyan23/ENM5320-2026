## Simple Noether then PE

- Consider particle w/position $q$, veloc.ity $\dot{q}$, Luglengion 1 (q, $\dot{q}$ )
- Define manentum $p=\partial \dot{q} L$

Force $\quad F=\partial_{q} L$

- Euler Lagrange $\frac{d}{d t} \partial_{q} L=\partial_{q} L$
or $\quad \frac{d}{d t} p=F$
- Assume now $q=q(s) \rightarrow$ a parameter that shifts $q$ (e.g. Changing the coordinates)
- Lagrangion is alift invowint if

$$
\frac{d}{d s} L(q(q), \dot{q}(s))=0
$$

Then, if $C=\rho \frac{d}{d s} q(s)$

$$
\dot{C}=0
$$

Pf $\quad \dot{C}=\frac{d p}{d t} \frac{d q}{d s}+p \frac{d}{d t} \frac{d}{d s} q$

$$
\begin{aligned}
& =\frac{\partial L}{\partial q} \frac{d q}{d s}+\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot{q}} \frac{d^{2} q}{d t d s} \\
& =\frac{d}{d s} L=0
\end{aligned}
$$

(def of $\rho_{1} \frac{d \rho}{d t}$ from $E-L$ )
